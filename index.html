NightyCloud





People go to the park to relax, to walk, to talk, to engage with one another. The main goal of our project is to understand park usage and perceived safety and seek to enhance the experience in a park while simultaneously addressing the need for public safety and an empirical way to measure it. We want to capture if illuminating areas in the park containing low light visibility with fun, interactive, and playful feedback loops can enhance park engagement and public safety. The audience of the project will be the NYC parks, who are in charge of the design and maintenance of the parks. By sensing the presence, movement and moving pattern of people, we can answer the research question that: 1) does illuminating low light visibility in park spaces with interactive devices make people feel safer? 2) will the interactive device increase public engagement in a park? Sensors embedded in an interactive device called Cloudy Night will be applied in locations with low afternoon and evening illuminances with potentially low visual access throughout the park. When people come close to the cloud, the light inside will change color, providing feedback to reflect human movement. Movement will be recorded by the Human Presents Sensor and stored remotely on the SD board. We will place this installation in different locations throughout the park to measure engagement (preferably in the afternoons and evenings). The data will then be used to analyze park engagement relative to time and location and visualized on an interactive map in a time-series.
